Goals of the project

The NHS is busy designing new digital services to help patients take control of their own health and care needs.

GP online services already offer patients the ability to do certain transactions online, including book GP appointments, order repeat prescriptions and access information in their GP record.

Currently, there are many ways to access these online services. The NHS is testing whether patients find it easier and more straightforward to access all these services in one place.

Therefore the NHS aims to transform the patient journey by enabling patients to stay on www.nhs.uk to access their online GP services. By doing this, we hope to improve patients experience and gain valuable insight in to several key areas:

  • The value and impact of the NHS brand on online services
  • The barriers to entry and use of the new process to resolve these
  • The ways in which different features on the product are used

The findings might also help us improve the existing services.

What are we doing?

NHS Digital has designed a pilot GP online service which is on www.nhs.uk.This provides patients with the ability to access their GP record, book an appointment, and order repeat prescriptions.

The pilot is now live in a small number of GP practices around the country. It will improve NHS Digital’s understanding of patient needs and help the NHS assess the experience of our services.

During this stage, feedback will be gathered on patients’ experience of accessing their GP record, and other GP online services.

The future

Once the feedback has been reviewed, the learnings will be used to help design future services. NHS Digital will then share the learnings from the project with a wider audience, including any relevant research that has been done.
